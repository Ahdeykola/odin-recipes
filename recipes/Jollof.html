Jollof

Description

Jollof rice is a popular West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, onions, and a blend of flavorful spices. It's commonly served at parties and family gatherings and is known for its rich taste and vibrant color. This recipe is a simple, delicious version of Jollof rice that you can easily make at home.

The origins of Jollof rice are traced back to the Nigerian region, and it has spread across several West African countries, each with its variations. Whether served with chicken, fish, or vegetables, Jollof is a must-try for anyone who loves bold, spicy flavors.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, blended
  • 4 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 2 whole tatashe, stem removed, seeded and roughly chopped
  • 4 whole ata rodo, stem removed
  • 2 whole shombo
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1 tablespoon minced ginger
  • 2 teaspoons thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 4 seasoning cubes
  • Salt to taste
